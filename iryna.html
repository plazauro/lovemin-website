The Heartbreak That Started A Mission.

Iryna Zarutska came to the United States full of hope. She was a vibrant young woman—a beloved daughter, a cherished friend—building a future while holding her family in Ukraine close to her heart.

Tragedy struck in the most senseless way imaginable. While traveling on a bus, Iryna was the victim of a violent crime—murdered in an act of random violence that cut her promising life short. The devastation for her family back in Ukraine was absolute.

In the midst of their paralyzing grief, her father faced a nightmare no parent should ever endure. He wanted to come to America. He wanted to see his daughter one last time. He wanted to bury his child.

The visa was denied.

While friends gathered around her graveside in America, her father was thousands of miles away in Ukraine, weeping into a phone. The bureaucracy saw "flight risk"; we saw a brokenhearted father denied his final human right of closure.

We vowed that day: Never again. The Iryna Zarutska Project exists to fight the battles grieving families cannot fight alone. We handle the visas, the travel, and the costs so that love can bridge any distance.

Turning Pain Into Reunion

We honor Iryna's memory by removing the barriers that keep families apart during their darkest hours.

Visa Advocacy

We work with immigration lawyers to expedite visa approvals for emergency situations, ensuring families can travel when it matters most.

Travel Logistics

Flights are expensive. We fund emergency travel for Ukrainian families reuniting with loved ones in the US or Europe.

Reunification Support

Once families are together, we help them settle. Finding housing, community, and support systems so they can heal together.
